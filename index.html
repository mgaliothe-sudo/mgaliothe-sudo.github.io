-Knicks Legends

John Starks was a former professional basketball player, best known for his tenacious playing style as a shooting guard for the

New York Knicks in the 1990s. He was an NBA All-Star in 1994 and the recipient of the Sixth Man of the Year award in 1997. A popular

and electrifying player, he was also named to the NBA All-Defensive Second Team in 1993.

Patrick Ewing is a Jamaican-born American basketball player, widely considered one of the greatest centers in NBA history. He played 15 of

his 17 seasons with the New York Knicks, leading them to the NBA Finals twice, and won the NBA Rookie of the Year award in 1986. Ewing is an

11-time NBA All-Star, a two-time Olympic gold medalist, and has been inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame twice.

Walt "Clyde" Frazier was a legendary American former professional basketball player known for his time with the New York Knicks. As a point

guard, he was a key player for the Knicks, helping them win two NBA championships in 1970 and 1973. Beyond his athletic achievements, Frazier was

also an influential fashion icon who helped popularize athletic shoes with the first major sneaker endorsement for the Puma Clyde.

-My Current Favorite Players

  • Jalen Brunson is a professional basketball player for the New York Knicks playing the guard position. He was drafted by the Dallas Mavericks in

    2018 after a successful college career at Villanova, where he won two national championships and was named the national player of the year. He is a

    two-time All-Star and two-time All-NBA Second Team selection.

  • Karl-Anthony Towns is a 7'0" tall professional basketball player for the New York Knicks, known for being the first round pick in the 2015 NBA draft

    and winning the 2016 Rookie of the Year award. He is a five-time NBA All-Star and three-time All-NBA team member who made history as the firstcenter to win

    the NBA Three-Point Contest in 2022. He played college basketball at Kentucky before being drafted by the Minnesota Timberwolves, where he spent nine seasons

    before being traded to the Knicks in 2024.

  • Mikal Bridges is a professional basketball player known for his exceptional defense, his consecutive games played streak, and his role on the New York

    Knicks. Drafted by the 76ers in 2018, he was traded to the Phoenix Suns on draft night and later moved to the Brooklyn Nets before joining the Knicks in

    July 2024. He has not missed a single game in his NBA career, earning him the nickname "the Warden" for his defense.

    Madison Square Garden (MSG) has been built four times in three different locations, with the first opening in 1879 and the current venue opening in 1968. The

    original venue was a roofless arena near Madison Square Park, named after President James Madison. Subsequent MSG buildings have been located at 26th Street

    and Madison Avenue and on 8th Avenue before the current one was built above the former Penn Station.
